Analysis

Analysis Carbon emissions have changed dramatically between American cities. Generally, the per capita carbon emissions of cities in the western United States are lower than those in the Midwest and southeast of the United States. The difference is dramatic: when Latin America's carbon emissions are a very low amount of 1.413 tons per capita, the carbon emissions of 20 cities in the United States are twice that, 2.87 tons per capita. As the world's largest carbon emitter, the per capita carbon emission of the United States is the highest in the world. The total annual carbon emissions from all sources in the United States are nearly 6 billion tons. The authors of the report attribute the huge differences in carbon emissions between American cities to the effects of intensive and compact urban development, weather and extensive use of public transport in the United States. However, the situation in Seattle is puzzling: Seattle is very cold and public transportation is poor, but its carbon emissions are relatively low. Scientists say this is caused by the use of zero emission hydropower in the northwest of the United States. However, the situation in heavy industrial cities in the United States where coal burning is serious is usually very bad, because burning coal to produce the same electricity emits more carbon than hydropower or even natural gas power generation.

China's independent contribution

China's independent contribution is a package plan, which includes both mitigation and adaptation, as well as its position and objectives in the global response to climate change. We have determined that China's carbon dioxide emissions will reach the peak around 2030 and strive to reach the peak earlier. In addition, we will strive to reduce the proportion of non fossil energy in primary energy by 60-65% in 2030 compared with that in 2005, and increase the forest stock by 4.5 billion cubic meters, which are some of our goals in mitigation.
