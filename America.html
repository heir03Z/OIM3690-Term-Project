Total carbon emissions

2020-5.9 billion tons

The United States, which ranks second, emits 5.9 billion tons of greenhouse gases every year. In addition, the per capita carbon dioxide emission of the United States reaches 19.58 tons per year, ranking second in the world after China.

 

Analysis

Carbon emissions have changed dramatically between American cities. Generally, the per capita carbon emissions of cities in the western United States are lower than those in the Midwest and southeast of the United States. The difference is dramatic: when Latin America's carbon emissions are a very low amount of 1.413 tons per capita, the carbon emissions of 20 cities in the United States are twice that, 2.87 tons per capita. As the world's largest carbon emitter, the per capita carbon emission of the United States is the highest in the world. The total annual carbon emissions from all sources in the United States are nearly 6 billion tons.
The authors of the report attribute the huge differences in carbon emissions between American cities to the effects of intensive and compact urban development, weather and extensive use of public transport in the United States. However, the situation in Seattle is puzzling: Seattle is very cold and public transportation is poor, but its carbon emissions are relatively low. Scientists say this is caused by the use of zero emission hydropower in the northwest of the United States. However, the situation in heavy industrial cities in the United States where coal burning is serious is usually very bad, because burning coal to produce the same electricity emits more carbon than hydropower or even natural gas power generation.

 

 

prevention

Climate change is a major challenge facing the world, which requires countries to respond at the policy level at an unprecedented speed and scale. To achieve the goal of completely curbing climate and environmental degradation, first of all, countries need to get out of the haze of the current new crown (covid-19) pandemic and economic collapse, which puts forward multi-level requirements for national policy-making departments. In this regard, the United States put forward the "zero carbon emission action plan (ZCAP)", which has made profound considerations for stabilizing domestic employment, economic innovation and coordinated development of the environment in the post epidemic era.
